California Government Code § 65915 - Density Bonus Law

When an applicant proposes a housing development within the jurisdiction of a city, the city shall grant the applicant a density bonus and other incentives or concessions as follows:

(1) For a housing development of five or more units where the applicant agrees to include affordable housing:

    (A) A density increase of 35 percent if at least 15 percent of the total units are affordable to households at moderate income levels.
    
    (B) A density increase of 50 percent if at least 20 percent of the total units are affordable to households at low income levels.
    
    (C) A density increase of 80 percent if at least 10 percent of the total units are affordable to households at very low income levels.

(2) The density bonus shall be calculated over the maximum allowable residential density under the zoning ordinance applicable to the project.

(3) The granting of a density bonus shall not be interpreted to require any other waiver or modification of development standards.

(4) In addition to the density bonus, an applicant shall be entitled to incentives or concessions as follows:
    - One incentive for projects with 10-20% affordable units
    - Two incentives for projects with 21-30% affordable units
    - Three incentives for projects with 31% or more affordable units

(5) This statute preempts local ordinances where they conflict with state density bonus provisions.

(6) The local government shall not apply any development standard that will have the effect of physically precluding the construction of a development meeting the criteria for a density bonus.

Affordable Housing Definitions:
- Very Low Income: 50% or less of area median income
- Low Income: 51-80% of area median income
- Moderate Income: 81-120% of area median income

Effective Date: January 1, 2021
Last Amended: January 1, 2024
