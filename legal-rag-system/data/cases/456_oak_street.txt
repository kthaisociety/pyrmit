Project: 456 Oak Street Multi-Family Development
Location: Palo Alto, California
Date: March 15, 2023

Applicant: Oak Street Developers LLC
Proposal: 22-unit apartment building on 1.5-acre parcel

Project Details:
- Address: 456 Oak Street, Palo Alto, CA 94301
- Parcel Size: 1.5 acres
- Proposed Units: 22 units (total)
- Affordable Units: 4 units at moderate income (18% of total)
- Building Height: 32 feet (3 stories)
- Parking: 44 spaces provided (2 per unit)

Zoning: R-2 (Multi-family Residential)
Base Density Allowed: 15 units/acre = 22.5 units maximum
Density Bonus Requested: Yes (15% affordable at moderate income)
Density Bonus Calculation: 15 units/acre × 1.35 = 20.25 units/acre × 1.5 acres = 30.4 units allowed

Planning Commission Hearing:
- Date: March 15, 2023
- Vote: 5-2 APPROVED
- Commissioners in favor: Anderson, Chen, Martinez, Rodriguez, Wu
- Commissioners opposed: Johnson, Smith

Public Comments:
- 15 speakers total
- 8 in support (focus on housing need)
- 7 opposed (concerns about density and traffic)

Conditions of Approval:
1. Provide 15% affordable units (4 units at moderate income) for 55 years
2. Submit traffic impact study before building permit
3. Comply with all building code requirements including Title 24 energy standards
4. Three public hearings required (completed)
5. Landscaping plan subject to staff approval
6. Construction management plan required
7. Affordable housing monitoring agreement

Timeline:
- Pre-application meeting: September 2021
- Application submitted: November 2021
- Environmental review (Categorical Exemption): March 2022
- Planning Commission hearing: March 2023
- Final approval: March 2023
- Total time from application to approval: 16 months

Community Response: Mixed, some opposition regarding increased density and traffic impacts. Supportive comments highlighted need for affordable housing.

Staff Recommendation: Approval with conditions
Staff Report Highlights:
- Project complies with state density bonus law
- Provides needed affordable housing
- Traffic impact deemed acceptable
- Design compatible with neighborhood

Final Outcome: APPROVED
Construction Status: Under construction as of December 2023
Expected Completion: September 2024
