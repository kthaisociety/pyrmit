Project: 321 Pine Street Affordable Housing Development
Location: Palo Alto, California
Date: September 20, 2023

Applicant: Community Housing Partnership
Proposal: 28-unit affordable apartment building on 1.3-acre parcel

Project Details:
- Address: 321 Pine Street, Palo Alto, CA 94301
- Parcel Size: 1.3 acres
- Proposed Units: 28 units (total)
- Affordable Units: 28 units at low income (100% affordable)
- Building Height: 35 feet (3 stories)
- Parking: 42 spaces provided (1.5 per unit)

Zoning: R-2 (Multi-family Residential)
Base Density Allowed: 15 units/acre = 19.5 units maximum
Density Bonus Requested: Yes (100% affordable at low income)
Density Bonus Calculation: 15 units/acre × 1.50 = 22.5 units/acre × 1.3 acres = 29.3 units allowed

Planning Commission Hearing:
- Date: September 20, 2023
- Vote: 6-1 APPROVED
- Commissioners in favor: Anderson, Chen, Martinez, Rodriguez, Wu, Johnson
- Commissioner opposed: Smith (concerns about parking)

Public Comments:
- 22 speakers total
- 18 in support (strong community support for affordable housing)
- 4 opposed (parking and traffic concerns)

Conditions of Approval:
1. All 28 units affordable at low income for 99 years
2. Submit comprehensive traffic management plan
3. Parking waiver granted (reduced from 56 to 42 spaces per state law)
4. Three public hearings required (completed)
5. Green building certification (LEED Silver minimum)
6. Construction management plan with strict hours
7. Annual compliance reporting to city
8. Partnership with local transit agency for resident passes

Timeline:
- Pre-application meeting: January 2022
- Application submitted: April 2022
- Environmental review (CEQA Exemption - affordable housing): August 2022
- Design review: December 2022
- Planning Commission hearing: September 2023
- Final approval: September 2023
- Total time from application to approval: 17 months

Funding:
- Low-Income Housing Tax Credits
- City affordable housing funds
- County housing trust
- State HCD funding

Community Response: Strong support from housing advocates and community groups. Some neighbor concerns about parking addressed through transit partnership.

Staff Recommendation: Approval with conditions
Staff Report Highlights:
- Addresses critical affordable housing need
- 100% affordable at low income
- Qualifies for state density bonus and parking reductions
- Sustainable design features
- Near public transit

Final Outcome: APPROVED
Construction Status: Breaking ground February 2024
Expected Completion: January 2025
Estimated Cost: $18.5 million
